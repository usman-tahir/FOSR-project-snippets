Roles of Nitrogen and Phosphorous in River Health

Dissolved Oxygen is required for the animals in the river to live Catfish, Bass, Trout all require this Oxygen in varying concentrations. If the Oxygen is used up by the decomposition of organic matter this can create a dead zone where all of the animal life has either fled or died.

Nitrogen and Phosphorus are required by all living things, but too great a concentration can lead to algal blooms. When algae blooms die they can choke out the other life in the system as the oxygen in the water is used up by bacteria decomposing the algae. In a normal healthy river the amount of these nutrients is enough to promote some plant and algae growth which is necessary to feed the herbivorous animals that live in the water, but not enough to cause algae blooms.

Turbidity(clarity) when the water contains high amounts of sediment or algae this can block sunlight from getting to aquatic grasses and other plants at the bottom of the river. Sediments upon settling out can also choke out bottom dwellers.

List of Indicators

Indicator Description
Dissolved Oxygen (DO)
  • Catfish, Trout, Bass and underwater species require oxygen
  • If the dissolved oxygen is too low (dead zone) aquatic species become stressed, evacuate the area, or die
Nutrients
  • Nutrients, primarily in the form of nitrogen and phosphorus, enter our waterways through fertilizers (farms and lawns), failing septic systems, and wastewater treatment plants
  • Nutrients in excess can cause harmful algae blooms
Clarity
  • Sediments washing off the land and algae blooms reduce water clarity
  • Muddy waters block out sunlight, preventing growth of aquatic grasses
  • Excessive sediments fall to the bottom and can smother bottom dwellers
Algae
  • Algae blooms are caused by excess nutrients
  • Algae clouds the water and blocks out sunlight for aquatic grasses
  • Decomposing algae depletes the water of oxygen
  • Some algae can be harmful to human health
